**(3.1.1)** A metric space $(X,d)$ consists of $X \not= \emptyset$ and $d: X \times X \rightarrow [0, \infty)$, such that:

$$
\begin{align}
& (1) \quad \forall x,y \in X \ : \ d(x,y) \geq 0 \quad \land \quad d(x,y)=0 \iff x = y \\ \\
& (2) \quad \forall x,y \in X \ : \ d(x,y) = d(y,x) \\ \\
& (3) \quad \forall x,y,z\in X \ : \ d(x,y) \leq d(x,z) + d(z,y)
\end{align}
$$
